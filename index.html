Project Background

Environmental hazards—landslides, flooding, land degradation—pose increasing risks to vulnerable communities and ecosystems. Addressing these challenges requires integrated spatial analysis that combines terrain data, environmental variables, and remote sensing insights. As a GIS specialist, I focus on developing geospatial models and analyses that support evidence-based decision-making in hazard assessment, land use planning, and natural resource management. My work translates complex spatial data into actionable intelligence for government, academic, and conservation organizations.

The Challenge

Hazard Vulnerability Assessment
Identifying high-risk zones and understanding spatial patterns of environmental hazards.
Data Fragmentation
Integrating multi-source datasets (terrain, imagery, environmental variables) into coherent spatial models.
Evidence-Based Planning
Converting spatial analysis into decision-support outputs for planners and policymakers.
Validation & Accuracy
Ensuring spatial models are rigorously tested and validated against field data.

Project Objectives

  • Conduct multi-criteria spatial modeling to identify hazard-prone and resource-rich zones
