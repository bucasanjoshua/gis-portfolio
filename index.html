Project Background

Environmental risks continue to increase across vulnerable landscapes, requiring stronger spatial evidence for planning and mitigation. GIS, remote sensing, and terrain-based modeling provide a systematic foundation for understanding exposure, sensitivity, and land suitability. My professional work focuses on translating geospatial data into structured, decision-ready outputs for hazard management and environmental planning.

The Challenge

  • Hazard vulnerability across ecologically sensitive and populated areas remains high.
  • Data fragmentation across formats and sources limits integrated analysis.
  • Spatial decision support is often insufficient for local planning workflows.
  • Evidence-based planning requires transparent and reproducible GIS methods.

Project Objectives

  • Conduct spatial modeling for hazard and environmental assessment.
  • Analyze terrain and environmental variables relevant to planning decisions.
  • Develop GIS-based outputs that support land management and policy planning.
  • Validate results using field observations or secondary datasets.

Research Process

Field Data Collection

Compiled reference points, geotagged observations, and administrative records to ground spatial analysis in local context.

Remote Sensing

Processed imagery and derivative layers to extract vegetation, terrain, and environmental indicators for mapping workflows.

GIS Modeling

Applied overlay, reclassification, and index methods to generate interpretable geospatial outputs for planning support.

Validation & Interpretation

Compared model outputs with reference information and interpreted results for actionable planning and communication.
